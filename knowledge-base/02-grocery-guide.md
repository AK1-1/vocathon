# Grocery Guide — Categories, Items, and Quantities

Use this to choose the correct **category** and to interpret **quantities** when a
caller adds a grocery item. The category MUST be one of exactly these six values:
`Produce`, `Dairy`, `Pantry`, `Bakery`, `Household`, `Other`.

## Category → common items (including Indian staples)

- **Produce** — fruits & vegetables: tomato, onion, potato, aloo, pyaaz, banana,
  apple, palak (spinach), bhindi (okra), dhania (coriander), mirchi (chilli), lemon, ginger, garlic.
- **Dairy & Eggs** — milk, doodh, curd / dahi, paneer, butter, ghee, cheese, eggs.
- **Pantry & Grains** — atta (wheat flour), rice / chawal, dal (lentils), sugar / cheeni,
  salt / namak, oil / tel, masala & spices, tea / chai, coffee, biscuits, snacks, maggi, poha.
- **Bakery** — bread, pav, buns, rusk, cake, cookies.
- **Household & Cleaning** — detergent, soap, shampoo, toothpaste, phenyl, dishwash,
  toilet cleaner, tissues, garbage bags, agarbatti.
- **Other** — anything that does not clearly fit above, or when the caller is unsure.

If the category is genuinely unclear, use `Other` rather than guessing wildly.

## Quantity conventions

- Keep the caller's units: "2 litres", "1 kg", "500 grams", "half kg" → "0.5 kg",
  "ek packet" → "1 packet", "dozen" → "12", "do" → "2".
- If no quantity is given, use `unspecified` (do not assume a number).
- Common Indian quantity words: *ek* = 1, *do* = 2, *teen* = 3, *paav* = 250 g,
  *aadha / aadhi* = half, *sava* = 1.25, *dhai* = 2.5, *packet*, *dozen*, *bunch* (saag/dhania).

## Examples

- "Add 2 litres milk" → item: Milk, quantity: 2 litres, category: Dairy.
- "Thoda dhania le aana" → item: Coriander (dhania), quantity: unspecified, category: Produce.
- "Ek kilo atta" → item: Atta, quantity: 1 kg, category: Pantry.
- "Phenyl khatam ho gaya" → item: Phenyl, quantity: unspecified, category: Household.
