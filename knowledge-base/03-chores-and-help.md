# Chores & Domestic Help Guide

Covers two actions: assigning **chores** to family members, and leaving
**instructions** for domestic help.

## Priority (applies to both chores and instructions)

Priority MUST be one of: `low`, `medium`, `high`.
- **high** — urgent / time-bound today, or words like "abhi", "turant", "immediately",
  "before guests arrive", "zaroori".
- **medium** — normal, the default when nothing signals urgency.
- **low** — "whenever", "no hurry", "koi jaldi nahi", "sometime this week".

If priority is not stated, use `medium`.

## Chores

A chore is a household task assigned to a **family member** (not paid help).
- Common chores: clean the kitchen counters, take out the trash, water the plants,
  vacuum the living room, do the dishes, fold laundry, change bedsheets, pay the
  electricity bill, refill drinking water, charge the inverter.
- **Assignee** is a family member's name (e.g. Achyut, Mom, Dad, Priya). If no one is
  named, use `Unassigned`.
- **Notes** capture timing or detail, e.g. "after dinner", "before 6 PM", "use the blue bin".

## Domestic help instructions

An instruction is for paid help. **Recipient** is one of: `Cook`, `Maid`, `Driver`,
`Gardener`, or `Help` (when unclear).

- **Cook** — meals and kitchen prep. Common Indian dishes: dal tadka, paneer butter
  masala, rajma chawal, aloo gobi, bhindi fry, sambar, idli, dosa, chapati/roti, biryani,
  poha, upma. Capture meal timing if given ("for dinner", "lunch ke liye").
- **Maid** — cleaning & housekeeping: sweep & mop (jhaadu-pocha), dust, wash utensils,
  clean bathrooms, laundry, change bedsheets.
- **Driver** — pickups/drop-offs and errands: "drop kids to school at 8", "pick up from
  station at 6 PM", "take car for servicing". Capture time and place.
- **Gardener** — water plants, trim hedges, mow lawn, repot.

## Examples

- "Achyut ko bolo kitchen saaf kare dinner ke baad" → chore; title: Clean the kitchen,
  assignee: Achyut, priority: medium, notes: after dinner.
- "Cook ko bolna aaj raat paneer banaye" → instruction; instruction: Make paneer for
  dinner tonight, recipient: Cook, priority: medium.
- "Driver needs to pick up my daughter from school at 3, it's urgent" → instruction;
  recipient: Driver, priority: high.
